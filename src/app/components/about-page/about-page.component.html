My Story

I grew up in a small village near Cologne in Germany. I spent most of my time building stuff with gears using LEGO Technic. I loved it! By getting my first computer, Computer Games joined the mix.

After graduating from High School, I thought about what to do in life. Knowing my passion for Tech, mechanics, and logical thinking, I decided to study Mechanical Engineering.

Towards the end of my studies, I got interested in the field of computational Engineering. So I chose a Master's Thesis in the field of fluid simulation (CFD).

Although I did get a good grasp of how to do a proper CFD-Simulation, how it worked under the hood was unanswered. Thus I wanted to learn about computer science.

I started learning Python, which seemed a good fit for an engineer. Following up, I jumped into algorithms and their foundation data structures. I recognized solutions should start on the drawing board and not in the code editor.

Digging deeper, I was astonished by many of the clever ways existing algorithms approached a problem. After several months of intensive studies, I chose to build a visualization web application using angular. Including three topics: Pathfinding algorithms, sorting algorithms, and Machine Learning.

Knife Making

When Covid-19 started, I took the opportunity to learn about knife making. The first attempt ended in a broken blade due to inappropriate heat treatment. Investing around 100 hours of grinding, sanding, and in professional heat treatment resulted in the pictured knife.

Making knives taught me a lot about planning, attention to detail and the rewards perseverance can withhold.
